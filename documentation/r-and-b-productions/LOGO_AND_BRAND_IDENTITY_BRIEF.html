Purpose of this document

This brief consolidates R&B Productions positioning, partnerships, and creative intent so designers and AI image generators (e.g. Nano Banana Pro) can produce an effective, on-brand logo and consistent visual language. It is not a legal contract; partnership credits reflect stated creative associations.

Creative direction

Mission: “We try to create soulful contents exploring local flavours and serve it to the audience all-round the globe.”

The logo and identity should feel authentic, emotionally resonant, and globally legible—rooted in place and culture without reducing the brand to clichés. Favour warmth, craft, and cinema-adjacent sophistication over generic “tech startup” coldness.

Reference context — Malayalam cinema excellence

For mood and quality bar (not as a named partner of R&B Productions), the Wikipedia article on Anwar Rasheed illustrates a trajectory of Malayalam cinema work: directing and producing films with both commercial and critical recognition—useful as a cultural reference for “soulful storytelling” and regional cinema reaching wide audiences.

Select all text in the box below to copy.

Professional logo design for “R&B Productions” — a digital entertainment company owned by Roy and Bindu.

Brand facts: Partnered with directors Dileesh Pothan and Nadirshah (Malayalam cinema), actor Fahadh Faasil, and scenarist Syam Pushkaran. Core activities: movie production, movie promotions, digital content creation, and distribution.

Brand mission: Soulful storytelling that explores local (Kerala / South Indian) flavours and reaches audiences worldwide.

Visual direction: Merge modern digital-media aesthetics with warm, cinematic, authentic craft. Avoid tacky film-reel or clapperboard clichés. Suggest subtle regional warmth through refined typography, organic curves, or a restrained colour story—never stereotypes or caricature.

Colour: Deep cinematic base (charcoal, midnight blue, or warm brown-black) with one accent (warm gold, burnt amber, deep teal, or terracotta). Must work on both dark and light backgrounds.

Typography / lockup: Include a clear wordmark “R&B Productions” or a monogram “R&B” plus “Productions” — highly legible at small sizes for social avatars and video end-cards.

Style: Vector-friendly, scalable, minimal, premium. Mood: soulful, contemporary, globally relevant, locally rooted.

Inspiration note (mood only, not literal likeness): The spirit of high-quality Malayalam cinema—narrative depth and commercial-artistic balance—without depicting any real person.

Deliver: primary horizontal logo, compact stacked version, single-colour monochrome version, and clear-space guidance.

Using the prompt in Nano Banana Pro & similar tools

  • Iterate: Run 3–5 variations; pick one direction, then refine.
  • Negative prompts: Add “no photorealistic faces, no celebrity likeness, no cluttered film reels, no neon gamer aesthetic” if the tool supports negatives.
  • Export: Request SVG or high-resolution PNG with transparent background for web and video.
  • Legal: Before public use, verify trademark availability and any talent / partner name usage with counsel.
